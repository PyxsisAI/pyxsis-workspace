# 🗣️ Hablar — Blended Spanish for Bethany

A speaking-first Spanish course that grows over time. Two tracks woven together:

- **Track A — Everyday Conversation** (daily life, social, travel)
- **Track B — Medical / Nursing Spanish** (patients, assessments, care) — ties to Bethany's RN work

**Starting point:** advanced beginner (knows some basic words)
**Goal:** conversational fluency + confident patient communication
**Method:** speaking-first, interactive, spaced repetition. Every module = learn → drill → speak → review.

---

## How to use this course (interactive)

Just text me any of these in our chat. I adapt to your level and keep score.

| Say this | What happens |
|----------|--------------|
| **"teach me module N"** | I walk you through the next lesson, step by step |
| **"drill"** | Quick vocab quiz (English ⇄ Spanish) from what you've learned |
| **"roleplay"** | Live conversation scenario (cafe, patient intake, etc.) — I play the other person |
| **"roleplay <place>"** | Situational practice for a specific place — e.g. "roleplay shopping", "roleplay restaurant", "roleplay directions" (see `situations.md`) |
| **"check: <your Spanish sentence>"** | I correct your grammar/word choice and explain |
| **"voice"** | Speaking practice — I send audio to repeat, OR you send me a voice note in Spanish and I check your pronunciation & grammar |
| **"review"** | Spaced-repetition review of words you've struggled with |
| **"how do I say ..."** | On-demand phrase + pronunciation |

Progress and tricky words are tracked in `progress.md`.

## Course Map

### PHASE 1 — Foundation (Modules 1–6)
1. **Sounds & Survival** — pronunciation rules, greetings, courtesy | Med: introducing yourself to a patient
2. **Ser, Estar & You** — to be, names, feelings, "how are you" | Med: "how do you feel?" pain basics
3. **Numbers, Time & Tener** — 0–100, age, time, to have | Med: age, pain scale 0–10, vitals numbers
4. **Present Tense Verbs** — regular -ar/-er/-ir verbs, daily actions | Med: "where does it hurt?" body parts
5. **Questions & Directions** — who/what/where/when/why, locations | Med: "show me where," room/clinic navigation
6. **Phase 1 Review + Conversation** — put it together | Med: full basic patient greeting & intake

### PHASE 2 — Building (Modules 7–12)
7. Past tense (preterite) — what happened | Med: symptom history ("when did it start?")
8. Future & plans — going to / will | Med: instructions, next steps, follow-up
9. Food, home, daily routine | Med: diet, daily living, mobility
10. Commands (formal usted) | Med: "breathe," "sit," "take this" — care instructions
11. Medications & quantities | Med: dosage, frequency, allergies
12. Phase 2 Review | Med: complete symptom assessment

### PHASE 3 — Conversational Fluency (Modules 13–18)
13. Opinions & feelings in depth
14. Storytelling (past + description)
15. Hypotheticals & polite requests
16. Family & emotional communication | Med: speaking with patient families
17. Full nursing assessment in Spanish
18. Capstone — sustained conversation + full patient encounter

### 🌎 SITUATIONAL SESSIONS — Everyday Survival (run anytime, see `situations.md`)
Practical, place-based Spanish you can use immediately — practice in parallel with the modules:
- Shopping · Restaurants · Café · Public facilities & directions · Transportation · Pharmacy & bank · Help & emergencies
- Plus universal phrases that work everywhere. Run with **"roleplay <place>"**.
