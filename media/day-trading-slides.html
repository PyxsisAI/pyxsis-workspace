Day Trading

Fundamentals, Risk & Discipline

What Day Trading Is

  • Buying and selling a security within the same day — no positions held overnight.
  • Profit comes from small, frequent price moves, not long-term growth.
  • Requires speed, focus, and a tested plan — it is a skill, not luck.
  • Most beginners lose money. The edge is discipline + risk control, not picking winners.

Key Terms

Liquidity

How easily you can enter/exit without moving the price.

Volatility

Size of price swings — your opportunity and your risk.

Spread

Gap between bid and ask. Tighter = cheaper to trade.

Volume

Shares traded. High volume = reliable moves.

Stop-Loss

Auto-exit that caps your loss on a trade.

Float

Shares available to trade. Low float = sharp moves.

The Golden Rule of Risk

1%

Never risk more than 1–2% of your account on a single trade.

  • $5,000 account → max loss $50–$100 per trade.
  • Survival first. You cannot trade tomorrow if you blow up today.
  • Set the stop-loss before you enter — never after.

Common Strategies

Scalping

Dozens of tiny trades, seconds to minutes. Needs tight spreads.

Momentum

Ride stocks moving hard on news/volume. Exit when it stalls.

Breakout

Enter when price clears a key level with volume behind it.

Reversal

Fade an overextended move. Higher risk, needs confirmation.

Pick one. Master it before adding another.

Your Toolkit

  • Broker — low fees, fast fills, good platform.
  • Charting — candlesticks, volume, a few indicators (don't overload).
  • Watchlist — 3–5 stocks you know well, prepped the night before.
  • Trade journal — log every trade: entry, exit, reason, lesson.
  • News feed — catalysts move stocks; know what's driving the day.

Mistakes That Kill Accounts

  • Revenge trading — chasing losses with bigger bets.
  • No stop-loss — letting a small loss become a huge one.
  • Overtrading — forcing trades when there's no setup.
  • Oversizing — risking too much on "the sure thing."
  • Trading on emotion instead of a written plan.

A Disciplined Daily Routine

  • Pre-market — review watchlist, news, key levels.
  • Plan — write entries, stops, and targets before the open.
  • Execute — take only setups that match your plan.
  • Cap it — set a daily loss limit; stop when you hit it.
  • Review — journal every trade. Find the pattern in your wins and losses.
