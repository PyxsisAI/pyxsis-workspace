Finance for Women 50+

5 Social Security Mistakes
That Cost Women Thousands

The decisions most women get wrong — and how to keep your money.

Why this matters for us

Women face this differently

  • We live longer on average
  • More likely to outlive a spouse
  • Often lower lifetime earnings

So these choices matter more — let's protect your income.

Mistake 1

Claiming too early at 62

~30% smaller check — forever

Full retirement age is 67. Waiting to 70 adds 8%/year — up to +24% for life.

Mistake 2

Overlooking the survivor benefit

The widow keeps the BIGGER check

Not both. If the higher earner delays to 70, those credits carry over to you for life.

Mistake 3

Not knowing the divorced-spouse rule

Married 10+ years?

You may claim on your ex's record. It doesn't reduce his benefit — and he's never notified.

Mistake 4

Misunderstanding the spousal benefit

50% of his FRA amount

Not his age-70 amount. And you get the higher of the two checks — never both.

Mistake 5

Missing Medicare at 65

Delaying Social Security ≠ delaying Medicare

Sign up ~3 months before your 65th birthday or face lifetime late penalties.

Quick recap

Your 5-point checklist

  • Don't auto-claim at 62
  • Plan the survivor benefit
  • Check your ex's record (10+ yrs)
  • Know the spousal cap
  • Medicare at 65
